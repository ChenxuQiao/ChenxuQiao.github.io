托福写作核心词汇宝典 (按话题分类)

引言

在托福写作中，词汇的广度和深度是取得高分的关键。然而，死记硬背单词列表往往效率低下，且难以在实际写作中灵活运用。本篇博文旨在为您提供一份按话题分类的核心词汇宝典，将单词置于具体的语境中，并结合高质量的范文展示其用法。通过这种方式，您不仅能记住单词，更能学会如何使用它们，从而让您的文章表达更精确、更地道、更具说服力。

i. 教育 (Education)

核心概念与培养目标

  • 培养 (动词): cultivate, foster, nurture (vt.) > 例: Schools should cultivate students’ critical thinking skills, foster a love for learning, and nurture their individual talents.
  • 促进学生身心发展: promote the student’s physical, mental (intellectual), and emotional development > 例: A well-rounded curriculum is essential to promote the student’s physical, mental, and emotional development.
  • 心理健康 (名词): psychological soundness, psychological well-being, psychological welfare > 例: Maintaining students’ psychological well-being is as important as their academic achievements.
  • 学习能力 (先天): aptitude, talent (n.) > 例: She has a natural aptitude for languages, while he shows a remarkable talent for music.
  • 学习能力 (后天): ability, skill (n.) > 例: Through practice, he developed the ability to solve complex mathematical problems and honed his analytical skills.
  • 学校教育 (名词): schooling > 例: Formal schooling provides the foundational knowledge necessary for higher education and career development.
  • 家庭教育 (名词): upbringing, parenting > 例: A child’s upbringing significantly influences their values and behavior, highlighting the importance of effective parenting.
  • 激励 (动词): motivate (vt.), give students motivation to do sth. > 例: Teachers should find creative ways to motivate students and give students motivation to explore new subjects.
  • 青少年 (名词): adolescents, youngsters, youths > 例: Understanding the challenges faced by adolescents is crucial for designing effective educational programs for these youngsters and youths.

知识传授与学习方法

  • 传授知识 (动词): impart, inculcate (vt.) knowledge > 例: Effective educators not only impart facts but also inculcate a deep understanding of concepts.
  • 灌输道德观念: instill high moral values > 例: Besides academic subjects, schools have a responsibility to instill high moral values in students.
  • 启发学生思考/给予灵感: stimulate students’ thought, give students inspiration > 例: The goal of a good lecture is to stimulate students’ thought and give students inspiration for further inquiry.
  • 教学法 (名词): teaching methodology, pedagogical methodology > 例: Different subjects may require varied teaching methodologies to achieve the best learning outcomes.
  • 适应 (动词): adapt to (doing) sth., adjust oneself to (doing) sth. > 例: Students must adapt to the new learning environment and adjust oneself to a more demanding academic schedule.
  • 适应能力 (名词): adaptability > 例: In a rapidly changing world, adaptability is a key skill for lifelong learning and career success.
  • 就业技能 (名词): employable skills, marketable skills > 例: Higher education should equip graduates with employable skills and marketable skills that are valued by employers.
  • 死记硬背 (名词/动词短语): rote learning, learn things by rote > 例: While rote learning can be useful for basic facts, it is often criticized for not fostering deep understanding.
  • 填鸭式教学 (动词): force-feed students > 例: Many argue that traditional methods often force-feed students with information rather than encouraging critical thinking.
  • 应用 (动词): apply (vt.) > 例: The true test of learning is the ability to apply knowledge to solve real-world problems.
  • 盲从 (副词): follow sth. blindly > 例: Critical thinking teaches students not to follow instructions blindly.

创造力、压力与个性发展

  • 限制/扼杀创造力 (动词): extinguish, stifle, constrain creativity (n.) > 例: An overly rigid curriculum can inadvertently stifle students’ creativity.
  • 产生不必要的压力: beget/create undue pressure > 例: Overemphasis on standardized testing can beget undue pressure on both students and teachers.
  • 塑造性格 (动词): mould (BrE) / mold (AmE) one’s character (vt.) > 例: Education plays a vital role in helping to mould one’s character.
  • 责任感 (名词): a sense of responsibility > 例: Group projects can help instill a sense of responsibility towards team members.

学习环境与互动

  • 独立思考 (动词短语): think independently > 例: The aim of higher education is to equip students with the ability to think independently.
  • 在理解基础上学习: learn things through understanding > 例: It is more effective to learn things through understanding rather than by rote memorization.
  • 课程总称 (名词): curriculum > 例: The school’s curriculum should be regularly updated to reflect societal needs.
  • 课外活动 (名词): extracurricular activities > 例: Engaging in extracurricular activities helps students develop social skills.
  • 团队精神 (名词): team spirit > 例: Participating in group activities fosters team spirit.

知识类型与教育层次

  • 理论知识 (名词): theoretical knowledge > 例: While theoretical knowledge provides a strong foundation, practical application is equally important.
  • 专才 (名词): specialist > 例: Many students choose to become a specialist in a specific field.
  • 全面发展的 (形容词): well-rounded, versatile > 例: The education system should aim to cultivate well-rounded individuals who are versatile.
  • 为社会福祉做贡献: contribute to societal well-being > 例: One ultimate goal of education is to empower individuals to contribute to societal well-being.
  • 学科 (通称): discipline, humanities, social sciences, sciences, engineering

托福作文范文 (教育)

Topic: Do you agree or disagree with the following statement? The primary purpose of education is to prepare students for a career, rather than to develop well-rounded individuals.

In contemporary society, the role of education is a subject of ongoing debate. While some argue that its main objective should be to equip students with employable skills for their future careers, I firmly believe that the primary purpose of education extends far beyond mere vocational preparation. Instead, it should aim to cultivate well-rounded individuals by fostering critical thinking, instilling high moral values, and promoting overall psychological well-being.

Firstly, an education system that solely focuses on career preparation risks producing specialists who may lack the broader adaptability required in a rapidly changing world. While theoretical knowledge in a specific discipline is important, the ability to think independently and apply knowledge in diverse contexts is paramount. If schools merely force-feed students information or encourage rote learning, they may stifle creativity and fail to stimulate students’ thought. True education should nurture innate aptitudes and develop critical skills that allow individuals to analyze complex issues, rather than simply memorize equations, formulas, theorems, and laws.

Secondly, education plays a crucial role in personal development. Its goal should be to promote the student’s physical, mental, and emotional development. Engaging in extracurricular activities, for instance, fosters team spirit and helps mould one’s character. Furthermore, a comprehensive curriculum that includes the humanities and social sciences allows students to develop a sense of responsibility towards society. This holistic approach ensures that adolescents grow into responsible citizens who can contribute to societal well-being, rather than individuals who follow things blindly.

Ultimately, while preparing students for employment is an important aspect of schooling, it should not overshadow the fundamental goal of developing individuals who are intellectually curious and ethically sound. An education system that successfully motivates students to learn things through understanding and encourages them to become versatile thinkers will better serve both the individual and society. Ignoring these broader aims may create undue pressure for immediate job relevance, at the expense of long-term growth.

托福作文范文 (科技与媒体)

Topic: Do you agree or disagree with the statement? The media (news organizations) are having a negative impact on society.

In the information age, media have become an undeniably pervasive force, shaping public discourse thanks to the proliferation of the Internet and electronic media. While the media’s vital role in disseminating information is indisputable, I contend that certain prevalent trends within modern news outlets are exerting a detrimental impact on society, primarily stemming from the tendency to sensationalize news and the potential for biased reporting.

One significant concern is the media’s tendency to exaggerate things, often driven by a pursuit of viewership. An emphasis on media hype frequently overshadows the commitment to objective and balanced reporting, consequently distorting public perception. For instance, the disproportionate focus on excessive violent content leads to a public that is inundated with fear-inducing narratives rather than factual accounts. The societal consequence is often an elevated state of anxiety and a skewed understanding of actual risks.

Furthermore, the challenge of maintaining objectivity remains a critical issue. Underlying economic and political pressures can lead to skewed or unobjective reporting. Some news organizations might excise dissenting viewpoints or strategically frame information to favor a particular agenda. This makes it difficult for citizens to find consistently reliable and trustworthy sources, especially when the online sphere is awash with misleading narratives and sometimes fraudulent information. This can severely undermine the media’s crucial role as a guardian of public interest.

It is, however, essential to acknowledge the positive contributions. Investigative journalism can uncover scandals, reveal systemic injustices, and provide up-to-date information on current affairs. The core problem arises not from the media itself, but from operational practices where the pressure to be entertaining or newsworthy can compromise journalistic integrity.

In conclusion, while an independent media is indispensable, its current trajectory, marked by sensationalism and bias, is a cause for concern. To mitigate these negative impacts, consumers must cultivate critical discernment, and news organizations must recommit to their foundational code of ethics, prioritizing truth over media hype.

托福作文范文 (旅游)

Topic: Some people say that tourism helps to promote understanding between countries. Others say that tourism causes tension between people from different cultures. What is your opinion?

The global tourism industry has brought diverse cultures into closer contact, sparking debate about its societal impact. While critics contend it can lead to conflict, I believe the benefits of tourism in bridging cultural divides often outweigh the negatives, especially when managed responsibly.

On one hand, tourism offers invaluable opportunities to gain first-hand experience of different ways of life, which can effectively broaden one’s horizons. When tourists respectfully interact with the locals, they move beyond stereotypes, leading to a more authentic appreciation of other cultures. Such meaningful interactions can build genuine empathy in a way that no second-hand experience from documentaries can. This demonstrates that even in a digital age, the Internet will not render tourism obsolete in its capacity for human connection.

On the other hand, it is undeniable that unchecked tourism can be a source of tension. An influx of visitors can strain local resources and damage fragile ecosystems. Furthermore, tourism can lead to the commercialization or even commodification of cultural heritage, causing resentment among local inhabitants. Certain resorts might seclude tourists from the locals, creating socio-economic enclaves that prevent meaningful interaction. The proliferation of exploitative tourist traps can further detract from an authentic visitor experience.

However, these negative consequences are typically symptomatic of unsustainable practices. Responsible tourism initiatives—which prioritize respect for local culture, ensure equitable economic benefits, and encourage genuine interaction—can effectively mitigate these issues. When host communities are empowered in the planning and management of tourism, the likelihood of conflict significantly diminishes.

In conclusion, while tourism carries the potential for cultural friction, its capacity to foster understanding and break down international barriers is immense. By emphasizing respectful and sustainable engagement, tourism can serve as a significant positive force, allowing people to expand their vision and appreciation of our shared global community.

托福作文范文 (政府)

Topic: It is more important for a government to spend money on improving infrastructure than on funding the arts. Do you agree or disagree?

The allocation of public funds is a perennial subject of debate. While both infrastructure development and arts funding contribute to the well-being of the citizenry, I largely agree that government spending on improving infrastructure should take precedence. This is because robust infrastructure forms the bedrock of economic vitality and public safety, which are fundamental responsibilities of any government.

Firstly, a well-maintained infrastructure is essential for a society’s daily functioning and economic prosperity. When authorities allocate money to these areas, they directly boost productivity and improve the quality of life for all citizens. Neglecting infrastructure can lead to economic stagnation and a lower standard of living. Therefore, governments must give priority to these foundational investments.

Secondly, while the arts enrich society, their funding can often be supported by private patronage and corporate sponsorships. The government’s primary obligation is to provide essential public services that the private sector is less likely to address, such as ensuring national security or supporting the unemployed. If a government is forced to curtail its budget, cutting back on arts subsidies is often less detrimental in the short term than underfunding critical infrastructure projects.

Of course, this does not mean the arts should be entirely neglected. A democratic and progressive government should strive to foster a culturally vibrant society. However, the scale of investment needs to be proportional to other pressing needs. A short-sighted policy would be to lavishly fund artistic endeavors while bridges crumble. The most prudent approach involves finding a balance, but when faced with limited resources derived from tax revenue, fundamental needs related to infrastructure must be the priority.

In conclusion, while both are valuable, the government’s core responsibility to ensure the economic well-being of its people makes spending on infrastructure a more critical priority. Strategic investment in these foundational elements creates a stable platform upon which other societal benefits, including a flourishing arts scene, can be built.

托福作文范文 (动物)

Topic: Some people think that the use of animals for medical experimentation is cruel. Others believe it is essential. Discuss both views and give your opinion.

The ethics of using animals in medical research is a deeply divisive issue. While some view the practice as inherently cruel, others argue it is vital for scientific progress.

Proponents of discontinuing animal experimentation, often including animal rights activists, highlight the suffering inflicted upon animals. They argue that procedures like vivisection are inhuman and inhumane. They question the translatability of findings from animals to humans, suggesting resources would be better invested in developing alternative research methods.

Conversely, many scientists maintain that animal research is indispensable. They contend that for many complex biological systems, there are no substitutes for live animal models. Historically, numerous medical breakthroughs have relied on animal experimentation. Supporters emphasize regulations in place to minimize suffering, such as the mandatory use of anesthetics and efforts to ease animals’ pain.

In my opinion, while the immediate abolition of animal testing might hinder critical medical research, society has an ethical obligation to vigorously pursue alternatives. The goal should be the eventual replacement of animal models wherever feasible. Until then, the use of animals must be strictly regulated, limited to essential research where no viable alternatives exist, and conducted with the utmost effort to alleviate animals’ pain. We must protect all living beings, especially endangered species whose habitats are already under threat from activities like poaching.

托福作文范文 (语言与文化)

Topic: The globalization of business means all countries should adopt English as their primary language for education and commerce. Do you agree or disagree?

The rise of globalization has fostered interconnectedness, with English often serving as a lingua franca. This has led some to argue that all nations should adopt it as their primary language. However, I disagree, as this would lead to the extinction of lesser-known languages, erode cultural diversity, and impoverish our collective human cultural heritage.

Firstly, mandating English would inevitably disparage indigenous languages and local dialects. Language is intrinsically linked to cultural identity, carrying the history and values of its speakers, passed down from their ancestors. If lesser-known languages become extinct, a wealth of knowledge is lost forever, impacting not only direct descendants but all of posterity.

Secondly, the widespread adoption of English could undermine harmony between ethnic groups. For ethnic minorities, their vernacular is a cornerstone of their community. Forcing them to abandon their mother tongue could lead to alienation rather than promoting healthy cultural integration and synthesis. True progress lies in multilingualism, which enriches societies.

While the proliferation of English offers benefits, it does not require abandoning other languages. Many countries successfully navigate globalization while preserving their own languages, often by promoting bilingualism. This allows citizens to participate in the global sphere while maintaining their distinct cultural identity and time-honored traditions. Culture is not static, and embracing global communication does not necessitate the deprecation of local languages.

In conclusion, while English plays an important role as a universal language, its adoption as the primary language for all would be detrimental to global cultural diversity. Fostering an environment where multiple cultures can have positive interaction is a far more enriching path.

托福作文范文 (发展)

Topic: The rapid pace of modern life and increasing urbanization are largely negative developments for individuals and society. To what extent do you agree or disagree?

In recent decades, the tempo of life has accelerated, particularly within expanding urban centers. While this era of intense urbanization has yielded benefits, I largely agree that its overall impact has been predominantly negative, leading to increased stress and alienation between people.

One of the most significant drawbacks is the impact on individual well-being. The relentless pressure to succeed in a materialistic society, coupled with navigating traffic congestion, contributes to chronic fatigue and stress. This environment can impede personal growth and lead to health issues like depression and insomnia. Many individuals find little time for leisure, and this constant pressure can be counterproductive.

Furthermore, rapid urbanization can exacerbate social inequalities and strain public facilities. As cities grow, the disparity between the city and the countryside may widen. This can lead to overcrowded living conditions and inferior public services for poverty-stricken populations living alongside affluent ones. The focus on constructing new skyscrapers sometimes comes at the expense of preserving buildings of historic significance.

Of course, urbanization drives economic growth, offering access to a diverse labor pool and fostering innovation. However, this pursuit should not overshadow the need for a healthy social fabric. While options to telecommute might help, the underlying status quo often prioritizes relentless development over human well-being. A broader strategy must address issues like poverty alleviation and mental health.

In conclusion, while modern development offers advantages, its current trajectory presents significant negative consequences. A shift towards more human-centered development is needed to ensure progress truly serves the well-being of all citizens, rather than just the goal to pursue maximum profit.

  • 不可再生资源 (名词): non-renewable resources (e.g., fossil fuels)

  • 白色污染 (名词): non-biodegradable garbage

  • 臭氧层 (名词): ozone layer

托福作文范文 (环境)

Topic: Some believe economic development is too important to be hindered by environmental concerns. Others argue environmental protection must be prioritized. Discuss both views.

The relationship between economic development and environmental protection is a critical debate. While both perspectives have merit, I believe that environmental protection must be integrated into, and considered a prerequisite for, true sustainable development.

Proponents of prioritizing economic development argue that it is necessary to improve living standards. From this viewpoint, environmental regulations can be seen as burdens. This perspective can lead to the over-exploitation of natural resources and practices that pollute air and water, assuming damage can be fixed later. However, this ignores that non-renewable resources are finite and damage can deteriorate to an irreversible state.

Conversely, environmentalists argue that the environment is the foundation upon which economies are built. Activities like deforestation, the discharge of toxic waste, and burning fossil fuels not only wreak havoc on natural resources and biodiversity but also undermine long-term economic stability. Global warming, driven by the greenhouse effect, poses a grave threat to agriculture and infrastructure. Failing to preserve the environment is economically irrational.

In my opinion, the idea that these two are mutually exclusive is a false dichotomy. True progress lies in pursuing sustainable development, which involves investing in renewable resources and promoting eco-friendly technologies. We must condemn rather than condone practices that damage the ecosystem. Raising public awareness is also crucial. Economic development that depletes resources and contaminates the environment is not development but a temporary gain that will aggravate problems in the long run.

ix. 全球化 (Globalization)

  • 文化特性 (名词): (national/regional) cultural identity
  • 全球文化同质化 (名词): global cultural homogenization
  • 文化同一性 (名词): cultural homogeneity/uniformity
  • 全球化是一把双刃剑: Globalization is a double-edged sword.
  • 地球村 (名词): the global village
  • 席卷一切的趋势 (名词): sweeping trends
  • 融合东西方文化: synthesize Asian and Western cultures
  • 侵蚀/削弱 (动词): erode
  • 普遍的 (形容词): universal

托福作文范文 (全球化)

Topic: Do you agree or disagree? Globalization is primarily a negative force concerning its impact on cultural identity.

Globalization, one of the most sweeping trends of our time, has transformed the world into a global village. While it presents challenges, I believe globalization is a double-edged sword and not a primarily negative force.

One primary concern is that globalization leads to global cultural homogenization. The argument is that dominant Western media and corporations can erode local cultures, resulting in cultural uniformity where unique customs are replaced by a universal consumer culture. This suggests a loss of distinct cultural identity.

However, this view oversimplifies cultural exchange. Globalization also provides opportunities for cultures to share their unique attributes with a wider audience. The internet allows for the preservation and promotion of minority languages and traditions. Furthermore, many cultures actively synthesize global influences with their own, creating new, vibrant forms of expression. This suggests globalization can also be a catalyst for cultural innovation.

Increased interaction can also lead to greater understanding and tolerance. While the risk of cultural erosion is real, active engagement with global trends can strengthen, rather than diminish, a sense of local pride and cultural identity, as communities make conscious choices about what to adopt, adapt, or preserve.

In conclusion, while fears of global cultural homogenization are valid, globalization itself is not inherently negative. The ultimate impact depends on the agency of local communities to engage critically with global influences, preserving their heritage while embracing innovation.

托福作文范文 (家庭)

Topic: Discuss the challenges to maintaining strong family relationships in modern life and suggest ways to overcome them.

The family unit, a source of individual sense of belonging, faces complex pressures in the modern era. While the ideal of strong family bonds remains cherished, contemporary lifestyles challenge these connections. Understanding these hurdles is the first step towards fostering resilient families within an enlightened society.

One challenge is economic demand. In both affluent and deprived families, the necessity of dual incomes often means parents work long hours, reducing quality interaction with their offspring. Parents can feel tied down by responsibilities, and financial stress can lead to personality clashes.

Another challenge is the widening generation gap, amplified by technology. Elderly people may find it difficult to relate to the digital experiences of younger family members. Differing perspectives on issues like gender equality regarding the distribution of household chores can cause friction.

To overcome these challenges, families must make conscious efforts. Prioritizing quality time, free from digital distractions, is crucial. Addressing the generation gap requires mutual respect and a willingness to listen. In serious cases like domestic violence or addiction to drugs, seeking professional help is vital. Ultimately, fostering strong family ties requires commitment and communication to maintain harmony.

  • 猖獗的/泛滥的 (形容- 犯罪 (动词短语):** commit a crime
  • 严厉的 (形容词): stringent (e.g., laws/punishment)
  • 惩罚 (名词): punishment
  • 犯罪率 (名词): the crime rate, the incidence of crime
  • 猖獗的/泛滥的 (形容词): rampant
  • 违法 (动词短语): violate/break the law
  • 遏制 (动词): curb (vt.)

托福作文范文 (犯罪)

Topic: Some believe the best way to reduce crime is longer prison sentences. Others think there are better alternatives. Discuss both views.

Reducing the crime rate is a persistent societal concern. While one solution is longer prison sentences, others argue that alternative approaches are more effective.

Proponents of longer sentences argue that such stringent punishment acts as a deterrent. The fear of lengthy incarceration might make individuals hesitate before they violate the law. This approach ensures public safety by keeping those who commit a crime off the streets, which is especially important when crime is rampant.

However, others contend this is a limited approach. They argue that prisons can be “schools of crime” and that long sentences do not address the root causes of crime, such as poverty or lack of education. Alternative strategies focus on prevention and rehabilitation. These methods seek to address the underlying factors that lead individuals to commit offenses, thereby reducing the incidence of crime more sustainably.

In my opinion, while stringent laws and appropriate punishment are necessary, an over-reliance on longer sentences is not the most effective strategy. A more holistic approach that balances punishment with robust preventative and rehabilitative measures is likely to yield better results. Investing in social programs and rehabilitating offenders can significantly curb crime in the long term by addressing its multifaceted causes.
